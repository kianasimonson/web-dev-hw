Invisible

I love every book I have read from James Patterson. He really knows how to make a mystery so entertaining. I always have trouble putting his books down. I understand this book is also written by David Ellis but I wanted to focus on James Patterson because I admire his work and know more about it.

James Patterson started writing at the age of nineteen from Newburgh, New York. Before becoming a writer he was set on track to become a professor and decided this wasn't the lifestyle for him. Invisible is an unbelievale book with a plot twist that will blow your mind. I always ponder on how authors come up with ideas such as these, but their imagination has to be wild.


"I POP awake, gasping for air, the dancing flames on the ceiling receding to a dark, quiet room. I wipe sweat from my eyes with the conforter and shake away the remnants of the familiar nightmare, only this time with a twist: this time, the person in the bed wasn't me. This time, the person in the bed was prettier than me, smarter than me, braver than me, older than me. This time, the person in the bed was Marta."

Patterson, James, and David Ellis. “Invisible.” Invisible, WF Howes Ltd, 2015, p. 25.
